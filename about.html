Free. Perpetual. Validated to primary authority. A pro bono contribution from tk.cpa to the taxpaying public.

Tax law belongs to everyone. The Internal Revenue Code is public. Treasury regulations are public. Court decisions are public. The law is not behind a paywall - but understanding it has always required either money, access, or luck.

CPA Validated exists to change that equation. Every tool, every guide, every resource on this site is free. No subscription. No account. No advertising. No conditions. The only requirement is that you use it well.

This is a pro bono contribution from tk.cpa to the public. A gift from a professional practice that believes tax law belongs to everyone - built to the same standards applied to paying clients, offered without charge, and committed to accuracy that would hold up under professional scrutiny.

Why This Exists

Tax resources exist on a spectrum. At one end: IRS.gov and the US Code - authoritative, but dense and difficult to navigate without training. At the other: blog posts, YouTube videos, Reddit threads - accessible, but often wrong, always uncited, never accountable.

In between, there is almost nothing. Professional research platforms cost thousands of dollars per year. Tax attorneys and CPAs charge hundreds per hour. The people who most need clear, accurate, cited tax information are often the least positioned to access it.

CPA Validated occupies that gap. Every result here traces to a specific IRC section, Treasury regulation, Revenue Ruling, or primary authority. Not because it is legally required - it is not - but because that is the only standard that holds up when the answer is challenged. It is the standard applied at Big Four firms and in serious tax practice. It is the only standard worth applying.

The Commitments

Always Free
Every tool and guide on this site will remain free, without account requirements, without advertising, and without paywalls. This is a commitment, not a pricing strategy. CPA Validated is a community resource - a pro bono contribution from tk.cpa to the taxpaying public. There is no version of this that costs you anything. Ever.
Always Cited
No position will be taken without a cited source. Every tool result, every article paragraph, every threshold and rate will cite the specific IRC section, Treasury regulation, Revenue Procedure, or court case that supports it. If we cannot cite it, we will not state it.
Always Current
Inflation-adjusted thresholds are updated every January when the IRS publishes new figures. When legislation passes that affects these tools, the affected pages are updated. This site is actively maintained by the tk.cpa AI lab. The commitment to keep it current is indefinite - not contingent on any subscription, platform, or revenue.
Always Honest
Where the law is unsettled, we say so. Where a position is aggressive, we label it. Where a tool's results require professional review before action, we say that too. This site will never overstate certainty to appear more authoritative than it is.
Always Accountable
A named, licensed practitioner stands behind every resource on this site. NY CPA #109025. FL CPA #AC58472. IRS EA #93250. These are real licenses issued by real regulatory bodies. They are verifiable through primary sources. Anonymity is not part of this project.

The Standard

Primary Authority
Every position cites the IRC section, Treasury regulation, Revenue Ruling, or court case that supports it. Not a summary of it - the actual source.
Practitioner Grade
Built to the same standard applied at Big Four firms. The same standard used on returns that get signed and filed with the IRS.
No Black Boxes
Every calculation is transparent. Every determination is explained. You can verify the result independently - that is the point.

The Architecture

This site is built on static HTML hosted on GitHub Pages. There is no database, no server, no framework to maintain, no subscription to pay. The files are open to inspect. The hosting is free. The domain is prepaid for years.

This is a deliberate architecture for permanence. Most web resources disappear within years - databases go offline, SaaS companies fold, APIs change. Static HTML on GitHub does not. The files that exist today will still render correctly in a decade, on any device, in any browser, without maintenance cost.

The goal is for this to outlast its creator. A resource that continues to serve after the practitioner who built it has retired - still cited, still accurate, still free.
