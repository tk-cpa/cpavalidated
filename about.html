I built this because accurate tax information should not require a $500 consultation or a Bloomberg Tax subscription. The rules are public. The IRC is public. The regulations are public. But the plain-English explanations - the ones that actually tell you what the law means and what to do about it - have always been locked behind professional paywalls or buried in IRS publications written for lawyers.

CPA Validated is my attempt to fix that. Every tool, calculator, and guide here is built to the same standard I apply to client work: primary authority only, no fabrication, every number and threshold verified against the current source.

This is a pro bono contribution to the community. There is no charge, no account required, and no advertising. If you find it useful and want professional help with your specific situation, my practice is at tk.cpa.

The Standard

What "Validated" Actually Means

Every position on this site cites a specific primary authority - not "generally," not "according to IRS guidance," but a specific IRC section number, Treasury regulation paragraph, Revenue Ruling number, or IRS publication page. That is the standard I apply to client work and the standard I apply here.

When the law is uncertain or a position is aggressive, it is labeled as such. When something is a reasonable inference rather than settled authority, it is disclosed. When Congress changes the law - as it did with OBBBA in 2025 - the affected pages are updated with the new citations and effective dates.

The goal is not a substitute for professional advice. It is a foundation for informed conversations and independent verification - so that anyone, whether they hire a CPA or not, can understand the rule that applies to their situation.

Limitations

What This Is Not

This site provides general information, not legal or tax advice. Tax law is highly fact-specific. The same rule applies differently to a married couple filing jointly, a nonresident alien, a C-corporation, and a trust. The tools and guides here provide correct general principles and frameworks - they do not and cannot substitute for professional advice tailored to your specific circumstances.

No professional relationship is created by using this site. Results from calculators are estimates only. If you need advice specific to your situation - especially for international tax, business transactions, or complex compliance matters - work with a licensed professional.
