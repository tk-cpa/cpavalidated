Bonus Depreciation Phase-Down Guide (2026)

40% in 2026  •  OBBBA Update  •  What Qualifies  •  Section 179 Comparison
IRC §168(k) OBBBA (P.L. 119-21) IRC §179
← cpavalidated.com

Bonus depreciation allows businesses to immediately deduct a percentage of the cost of qualifying property in the year it is placed in service, rather than depreciating it over its MACRS recovery period. The TCJA set the rate at 100% through 2022. It has been phasing down since. OBBBA (P.L. 119-21) extended the 40% rate through 2025 and 2026 - a two-year extension from where the original TCJA schedule would have landed.

OBBBA (P.L. 119-21) - What Changed

Under the original TCJA schedule, bonus depreciation would have been 40% in 2025, 20% in 2026, and 0% in 2027. OBBBA extended the 40% rate to cover both 2025 and 2026, giving businesses an extra year at 40%. The 20% rate now applies in 2027, and the phase-out completes in 2028 unless future legislation extends it again. IRC §168(k) as amended by OBBBA.

Bonus depreciation is automatic. Unlike §179, you do not elect bonus depreciation in - you must elect out if you do not want it. The election out is made by asset class on a timely filed return (including extensions). If you miss making the election, you are stuck with bonus depreciation for that asset class in that tax year. Treas. Reg. §1.168(k)-2(f)(1).

Placed-in-Service Requirement

The placed-in-service date determines which year's bonus depreciation rate applies. Equipment ordered in December 2026 but not delivered and operational until January 2027 is 2027 property subject to the 20% rate - not the 40% 2026 rate. For large capital expenditures, the placed-in-service date requires documentation: delivery receipts, commissioning records, and evidence that the property is in a condition or state of readiness to be used for its intended purpose.

Alternative Depreciation System (ADS) Election

Taxpayers who elect the Alternative Depreciation System for a class of property are not eligible for bonus depreciation on that property class. Certain businesses are required to use ADS: tax-exempt use property, certain listed property used 50% or less for business, and property used predominantly outside the US. Floor plan financing and certain agricultural businesses also have ADS requirements. IRC §168(g).
