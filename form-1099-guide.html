1099-K Threshold Changes - Critical Update
The 1099-K reporting threshold has been repeatedly changed and delayed. Timeline: $20,000/200 transactions (through 2022) - $600 threshold enacted by ARP (P.L. 117-2) - IRS delayed to $20,000 (2022, Notice 2022-53) - IRS delayed again to $5,000 (2023, Notice 2023-74) - $5,000 applied for 2024 (Notice 2024-85) - $2,500 for calendar year 2025 reporting (issued in 2026) - $2,000 for calendar year 2026 and beyond (permanent under OBBBA P.L. 119-21). OBBBA superseded the ARP $600 threshold and permanently set $2,000 as the floor. No further IRS delay guidance needed - this is now statute. Platforms like Venmo, PayPal, Etsy, eBay, Airbnb, and StubHub are required to issue 1099-Ks under these rules. Income is taxable regardless of whether a 1099-K is issued.

E-file required at 10+ forms. Payors filing 10 or more information returns of any type must file electronically via the IRS FIRE system. This threshold was reduced from 250 to 10 by the Taxpayer First Act of 2019, effective for returns filed after December 31, 2021. Treas. Reg. 301.6011-2.
!
Backup withholding at 24%. If a payee fails to provide a correct TIN (or has been notified by IRS of underreporting), the payor must withhold 24% of payments subject to information reporting. Applies to 1099-MISC, 1099-NEC, 1099-INT, 1099-DIV, 1099-B, 1099-K. The payor is liable for the unwithheld amount plus penalties if they fail to withhold when required. IRC 3406. W-9 collection is the payor's protection.
!
Penalties for late or missing 1099s (2026 rates). Per-return penalties apply to payors. Under IRC 6721-6722: $60/return if filed within 30 days of due date; $130/return if filed after 30 days but by August 1; $330/return if filed after August 1 or not filed; $660/return for intentional disregard. Annual caps apply per payor. Small-business reduced caps available. The penalties are assessed separately for failure to file with IRS AND failure to furnish to recipient.
