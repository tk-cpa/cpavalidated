30-Day Deadline. No Extensions. No Exceptions.
The Section 83(b) election must be filed with the IRS within 30 days of the grant date. There is no way to make this election late. Once the deadline passes, it is gone. IRC 83(b)(2); Treas. Reg. 1.83-2(b).

Grant Information

Section 83(b) applies to property transferred in connection with services that is subject to a "substantial risk of forfeiture" (e.g., vesting schedule). ISOs and NQSOs are NOT restricted property at grant - different rules apply.

Amount paid for the shares. If purchased at FMV, the spread (and hence ordinary income) is zero. Many founders buy at par value = FMV.
Projected FMV per share when fully vested (typically 4 years). This drives the tax comparison.

How to File the 83(b) Election - Checklist

  • 1
    Draft the election statement. The IRS does not have an official form. The election must include: your name, address, and SSN; description of the property; date of transfer; tax year; nature of restrictions; FMV at transfer; amount paid; and a statement that you are making the election under IRC 83(b). Treas. Reg. 1.83-2(e).
  • 2
    File within 30 days of the grant date. Count carefully - the 30-day period begins on the transfer date, not the grant date in some agreements. If day 30 falls on a weekend or holiday, the next business day is the deadline. File as early as possible; do not wait.
  • 3
    Send by certified mail, return receipt requested. Mail to the IRS service center where you file your individual return. Keep the certified mail receipt and a copy of the signed election permanently. The IRS may lose it - your proof of timely filing is the receipt.
  • 4
    Attach a copy to your tax return. Attach a copy of the 83(b) election to your Form 1040 for the tax year in which the transfer occurred. Treas. Reg. 1.83-2(c). This is a reminder step - the election is valid upon filing with the IRS, not upon attaching to the return.
  • 5
    Provide a copy to the company. Give a copy to the company (employer/partnership) as required by Treas. Reg. 1.83-2(d). The company needs it for its own compensation records and W-2 reporting obligations.
  • 6
    Keep all copies permanently. The statute of limitations on the election itself does not expire cleanly - especially if the shares are sold many years later. Keep the election, the certified mail receipt, and your grant agreement indefinitely.
Authority: IRC 83(b) (election to include in gross income in year of transfer); IRC 83(b)(2) (must be filed no later than 30 days after transfer); Treas. Reg. 1.83-2 (election requirements, content, filing procedure); Rev. Proc. 2012-29 (IRS guidance on 83(b) election content requirements).
