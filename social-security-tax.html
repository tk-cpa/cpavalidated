Key rule: Up to 85% of Social Security benefits may be taxable depending on your "combined income" (AGI + nontaxable interest + 50% of SS benefits). The thresholds - $25,000 for single and $32,000 for MFJ - have never been adjusted for inflation since they were enacted in 1984. Most retirees now pay tax on their benefits.
