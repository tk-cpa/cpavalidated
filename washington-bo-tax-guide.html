Washington B&O Tax: Business and Occupation Tax Guide

Gross Receipts on Revenue - 1.5% Service Rate - $100K Economic Nexus - No WA Income Tax
RCW 82.04WAC 458-20WA DOR
← State and Compliance

Washington State has no corporate income tax and no personal income tax. What it has is the Business and Occupation (B&O) tax - a gross receipts tax on the privilege of doing business in Washington, calculated on gross revenue rather than profit. An out-of-state company selling services to Washington customers may have Washington B&O nexus with no physical presence in the state. For professional services firms, SaaS companies, and any business with Washington clients, the B&O tax is frequently overlooked precisely because there is no income tax to prompt attention to Washington compliance.

Washington B&O Key Numbers

Service and other activities rate: 1.5% on gross receipts. This is the catch-all rate covering professional services, consulting, SaaS, technology services, and most B2B services.

Retailing rate: 0.471%. Wholesaling and manufacturing: 0.484%.

Economic nexus threshold: $100,000 of Washington-sourced receipts in a calendar year triggers B&O registration and filing obligations for out-of-state businesses - no physical presence required.

What it taxes: Gross revenue, not profit. A business losing money on Washington operations still owes B&O tax on Washington gross receipts.

Service Income and SaaS at 1.5%

The service and other activities classification covers law firms, accounting firms, consulting, staffing, technology services, and SaaS. At 1.5%, a professional services firm with $5 million of Washington-sourced revenue owes $75,000 of B&O tax on those receipts regardless of its profitability on Washington engagements. For SaaS companies, the Washington DOR applies the service classification at 1.5% - and separately, Washington may also impose retail sales tax on SaaS subscriptions sold to Washington consumers. The B&O tax and retail sales tax are separate obligations: the B&O is a cost borne by the business, while the retail sales tax is collected from the customer and remitted.

Washington can impose both B&O tax at 1.5% and retail sales tax at 8.5-10.5% on the same SaaS subscription. A SaaS company with $2 million of Washington consumer revenue may owe $30,000 in B&O tax plus collect and remit $170,000-$210,000 in retail sales tax. These are separate registrations, separate filings, and separate tax types - both must be addressed independently.

No Deductions for Costs

Unlike income taxes, the B&O tax has no deductions for wages, rent, cost of goods sold, or other operating costs. The tax is on gross receipts. Specific statutory exemptions and deductions exist - for example, certain receipts from services performed outside Washington, certain financial services transactions, and specific industry exemptions - but they must be specifically authorized by statute. General business costs do not reduce B&O taxable receipts.

Credit for Taxes Paid to Other States

Washington provides a credit against B&O tax for income taxes, business and occupation taxes, and similar taxes paid to other states on the same receipts. This prevents complete double taxation when a Washington-based business also pays income tax in another state on the same activity. The credit is limited to the Washington B&O tax attributable to the receipts taxed elsewhere.
